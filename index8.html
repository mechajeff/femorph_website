Redefining accuracy in digital twin creation

Born at AFRL, Hardened at Pratt & Whitney, Ready for Production.

FEMORPH was originally developed at the Air Force Research Laboratory to enable robust shape morphing and simulation accuracy. It has been rigorously tested and applied within Pratt & Whitney’s industrial workflows, and is now ready for production-scale deployment.

Basic FEMORPH Process

1. Collect Geometry Data

Geometry data is collected using coordinate measuring machines and optical 3D scanners. These tools produce high-accuracy point clouds or surface meshes for each part.

2. Import into FEMORPH

The scan data and simulation mesh are imported into FEMORPH and prepared for alignment.

3. Align and Compare

The measured geometry and simulation model are aligned, and deviation plots are generated to show geometric differences.

4. Morph the Mesh

FEMORPH automatically morphs the mesh to match the as-measured geometry, eliminating the need for remeshing.

5. Analyze the Morphed Model

The morphed model is ready for simulation, allowing accurate structural or flow analysis that reflects real manufacturing effects.
